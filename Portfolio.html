The Family Medicine Portfolio

What is it?

The porfolio is a unique (and mandatory) project you create during your first and second years of residency to document your experience, presentations, module assignments, and rotation evaluation forms.

This is a file in which students will keep:

  1. All assignments from the completed modules
  2. Evidence of attendance of clinic session
  3. Evidence of attending CME sessions and a report of each session attended
  4. Reflections from rotations, modules and conferences

    5. How is it graded?

    1. Module assignments (80 marks)

      2. Each module will be given a mark. This will be determined by averaging the marks given for the assignments for that module. The marks for the individual modules will be then averaged, and converted to a mark out of 80 for the final Portfolio mark.

    2. Clinic sessions

      3. Clinic sessions will be assessed by means of an evaluation form that will record attendance and performance at clinic sessions.

    3. CME Sessions (10 marks)

      4. Evidence that the student has been attending well selected CME courses (both local and abroad), which have relevance to primary care (6 marks).

      Evidence that there has been learning beacuse of CME exposure (4 marks).

    4. Reflections (10 marks)

      5. Evidence that students reflect on his/her learning, trying to relate learning to previous knowledge and experience, and evidence that the student is using reflective pieces as an instrument for growth (6 marks)

      Evidence that the student has experienced positive, expected change because of this course - change in attitude towards medicine, their learning, patients, and the ehalth care system (4 marks)

    Tips

    • Begin working on it from orientation week
    • Update your portfolio at least at the end of each hospital rotation
    • Take photos of yourself on the job or interesting cases you see during each hospital rotation to include in portfolio
    • Make an extra copy of each hospital rotation evaluation to have as hard copy to insert in your printed portfolio
    • Plan ahead where you will print and bind you finished portfolio
    • Aim to complete your portfolio ahead of time to allow yourself to study for Mock Diploma exams (portfolio is due close to mock exams)
    • Portfolio drafts are usually due first week of March during second year of residency
    • Final Portfolio (printed and bound) is usually due 31st March during second year of residency
